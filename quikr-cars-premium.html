Introduction to Listing flow

The listing flow is one of the most crucial areas of classified experiences. It is used by individuals and businesses alike to add vehicles to the platform. Although we were having a redesign of the entire flow.

The premium flow is one of the most critical phase of monetization for the platform. Users can upgrade their ad to any of the premium offerings which would boost their position in the overall listing and other benefits.

About the project

The main objective was to improve paid listing adoption in order to increase Sell-side revenues.While we were simultaneously working on improving the Post Ad flow to increase overall Listing Conversion, multiple A/B Tests were carried out in parallel to optimize the Paid Listing funnel.
Additionally, the new Paid Listing UX was expected to handle the increase no. of Paid offerings options that the product was now able to support (3 in total versus 2 at the beginning of the change). The status quo iteration was having a simple comparison grid showcasing the relative advantages/disadvantages of the various paid listing options.

Old Design

Design iterations

The primary reason for the initial redesign was to add premium flow in the new responsive listing flow. The current tabular design already had many usability and product issues as highlighted. Also some UX issues limiting to first time individual customers there:

  1. Information hierarchy seemed broken, we needed to convince user about features and benfits rather than showing offerings
  2. Too much information was being shown
  3. The form had too much information which seemed confusing for individual customers
  4. A lot of details about offerings were not relevant to cars & bikes vertical

There were some iterations of the tabular design but they were eventually dropped. We then decided to test the current layout with a new design which highlighted the feature of the premium program in general and how we boost their listing.

Later iteration

We still thought that there was scope in improvement on our progressive presentation of our offerings.

  1. The checkbox approach seemed odd in our earlier iteration, they seemed more like opt-in rather than deliberate user action
  2. Clear call to action are needed for better conversion
  3. Mobile listings as a percentage of overall postings have been increasing

We further iterated simply on the background color of the section in mobile and switching the CTA from a primary action button to a secondary action button to avoid confusion. This further improved the conversions for the mobile flow.
